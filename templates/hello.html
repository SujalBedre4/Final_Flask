About Astronaut

Intro

An astronaut is a highly trained and equipped individual who serves as a commander or crew member on a spacecraft, participating in human spaceflight programs. The term "astronaut" originates from the Greek words "astron nautes," meaning "star sailor." Astronauts are responsible for conducting space missions, performing extravehicular activities (EVAs), and contributing to scientific research in space. To prepare for the physical and mental demands of space travel, astronauts undergo rigorous training, which includes a comprehensive program of physical conditioning, scientific and technical education, and simulation-based training

To become an astronaut, one must possess a strong foundation in scientific and technical knowledge, excellent physical and mental health, and a passion for the mission. Astronauts undergo rigorous training, which includes extensive medical exams, physical conditioning, simulation-based training, and continuous learning to prepare for the challenges of space travel.
